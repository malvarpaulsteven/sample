History

On May 22, 1923, the Rizal Institute came to existence in Taal, Batangas, founded by the brothers Quirino, Jose, Juan Ramon and Mariano Eleazar of Lucban, Tayabas (now Quezon). Stocks of the corporation were later said to civic-minded people of Taal and Lemery, among whom was Dr. Roman B. Montenegro, who become the school's first president and in whose memory the high school building is named.

Its primary purpose was to offer secondary building which the founders deemed was the need of the community at that time. As early as 1930-1931, complete secondary education was finally offered by the school with an enrolment of 212 students.
